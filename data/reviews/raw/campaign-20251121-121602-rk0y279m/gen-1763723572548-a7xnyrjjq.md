## Narrative Feedback

As someone who thrives on the social dynamics at the table and loves seeing systems work together to support collaborative storytelling, this rulebook speaks my language. The fiction-first philosophy isn't just stated—it's woven into every chapter, every example, every principle. I can already imagine sharing this with my groups and watching their eyes light up.

The Core Principles chapter is exactly what I've been looking for in a game text. "The Table Is a Creative Team" captures the collaborative spirit I've been trying to articulate to new players for years. The explicit statement that "The GM describes outcomes, not numbers" gives me the language I need to explain why narrative play matters.

What really resonates is how the system handles partial success. As both a GM and a player, I know that the most memorable moments happen in the gray areas—when success comes with costs or complications. The way this book frames those outcomes as "core story beats" rather than mechanical compromises is brilliant.

I especially appreciate the Ways to Play chapter acknowledging GMless and solo modes alongside traditional play. It shows the designers understand that collaboration can take many forms. The explicit support for hybrid styles matches how my groups actually play—we shift modes session to session based on who's available.

The character creation flow strikes a good balance between narrative and mechanical scaffolding. Starting with concept before attributes, then weaving in identity elements, goals, and personal threads—this matches how I naturally think about characters as social beings first, stat blocks second.

Where I see room for improvement is at-the-table usability during actual play. The principles are clear, but when I'm in the middle of a heated scene with multiple players contributing, I need quick reference material. The organization assumes you'll read sequentially, which works for learning but not for mid-session lookups.

## Overall Assessment

This is a rulebook designed by people who understand that tabletop RPGs are fundamentally social experiences mediated by rules, not rules occasionally interrupted by roleplay. For Socializer-type players and collaborative GMs, the philosophical alignment is nearly perfect.

The fiction-first framework isn't window dressing—it's structural. Every mechanical element serves the story, and the text consistently reinforces this priority. The emphasis on intent and approach as the starting point for resolution matches how my best sessions naturally flow.

My concern is practical deployment. The book excels as a teaching text for the design philosophy but needs supplementary quick-reference materials for active play. A GM screen with the core resolution flow, outcome tiers, and key principles would transform this from "excellent to read" to "essential at the table."

For my groups—especially the ones where we share narrative authority and rotate GM duties—this system offers validation and vocabulary for what we've been doing intuitively. It codifies collaborative play without constraining it. That's rare and valuable.

Rating: 8.25/10 overall. Strong philosophical foundation with room for improved quick-reference accessibility.
