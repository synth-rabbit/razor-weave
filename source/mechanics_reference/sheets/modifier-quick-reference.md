# Modifier Quick Reference

Fast lookup for all modifiers, bonuses, and penalties.

---

## Core Modifier Types

### Skill Bonuses
**From training and experience**:
- +0: Untrained
- +1: Basic training
- +2: Competent
- +3: Skilled
- +4: Expert
- +5: Master

**Apply to**: Relevant skill checks

---

### Situational Modifiers
**From circumstances**:
- **-4**: Severe hindrance
- **-2**: Moderate hindrance
- **+0**: Standard conditions
- **+2**: Moderate help
- **+4**: Significant help

**Apply to**: Specific checks based on situation

---

### Proficiency Bonuses
**From specialized training**:
- **+1 or +2**: When using relevant proficiency
- **Advantage**: When proficiency particularly applicable

**Apply to**: Checks involving proficiency

---

## When To Apply Modifiers

### Stack Everything Relevant
**Typical check might include**:
- Skill bonus (+2)
- Proficiency bonus (+1)
- Situational modifier (+2)
- Advantage (roll 2d6, take higher)

**Example**: Lockpicking with tools
- Base roll: 2d6
- Thievery skill: +2
- Lockpicking proficiency: +1
- Quality tools: +2
- **Total**: 2d6 +5

---

### Don't Stack Same Type
**Only one of each type applies**:
- One skill bonus (highest if multiple apply)
- One proficiency bonus (if multiple, player chooses)
- Situational modifiers stack unless same source

---

## Advantage and Disadvantage

### Advantage
**Roll 2d6, take higher**

**Grant when**:
- Favorable position or preparation
- Ally provides meaningful help
- Opponent at disadvantage
- Clever use of environment
- Relevant proficiency applies exceptionally well

**Examples**:
- High ground in combat
- Assisted by ally
- Perfect tools for job
- Target is distracted
- Extensive preparation

---

### Disadvantage
**Roll 2d6, take lower**

**Impose when**:
- Unfavorable position
- Improvising or unprepared
- Injured or impaired
- Hostile environment
- Active opposition

**Examples**:
- Fighting while prone
- Working in darkness
- Exhausted or wounded
- Rushed or time pressure
- Terrible tools

---

### Advantage/Disadvantage Interaction
**Multiple sources of same**: Doesn't stack, still just one
**Advantage + Disadvantage**: Cancel out, straight roll
**Can't have both**: If any of each, they cancel

---

## Condition Modifiers

### Physical Conditions

**Wounded**:
- Disadvantage on physical actions (Athletics, Combat, etc.)

**Exhausted**:
- Disadvantage on all actions

**Stunned**:
- Lose next action

**Prone**:
- Disadvantage on attacks
- Enemies have advantage (melee) or disadvantage (ranged) against you

**Restrained**:
- Cannot move
- Disadvantage on attacks
- Attackers have advantage

**Blinded**:
- Disadvantage on attacks
- Enemies have advantage against you

---

### Mental/Social Conditions

**Shaken**:
- Disadvantage on mental and social actions

**Frightened**:
- Disadvantage on all actions while source visible
- Cannot move closer to source

**Confused**:
- Actions may be random or GM-directed

---

### Beneficial Conditions

**Empowered**:
- Advantage on relevant actions

**Inspired**:
- Advantage on next check OR +2 bonus

**Hidden**:
- Advantage on attacks from hiding
- Enemies can't directly target you

**Defended**:
- +2 to defense OR attackers have disadvantage

---

## Combat Modifiers

### Positioning

**High Ground**:
- +2 to attack OR advantage

**Cover (Light)**:
- +2 to defense OR attackers disadvantage

**Cover (Heavy)**:
- +4 to defense OR attackers major disadvantage

**Flanking** (if used):
- +2 to attack OR advantage

**Prone**:
- -2 to attack OR disadvantage

---

### Range

**Close (melee or adjacent)**:
- No modifier

**Medium Range**:
- No modifier for ranged
- Disadvantage for thrown

**Long Range**:
- -2 to attack OR disadvantage

**Extreme Range**:
- Major disadvantage or auto-miss

---

### Tactical

**Surprised**:
- Lose first action
- Enemies advantage first round

**Outnumbered**:
- GM may grant advantage to multiple attackers

**Charging**:
- +2 to attack but may expose you

**Aiming** (ranged):
- Spend action to aim: +2 or advantage next attack

---

## Environmental Modifiers

### Lighting

**Bright**:
- No modifier

**Dim**:
- -2 to Perception
- Disadvantage on precise tasks

**Darkness**:
- -4 to Perception
- Major disadvantage on most actions
- Advantage for stealth

---

### Weather

**Clear**:
- No modifier

**Rain/Wind**:
- -2 to ranged attacks
- -2 to Perception

**Heavy Storm**:
- Disadvantage on ranged attacks
- Disadvantage on Perception
- Movement hindered

**Extreme** (blizzard, hurricane):
- Major disadvantage on most actions
- Possible damage or forced movement

---

### Terrain

**Open Ground**:
- No modifier

**Difficult Terrain**:
- Movement costs double
- -2 to Athletics or disadvantage

**Hazardous Terrain**:
- Risk of damage
- Checks to move safely
- Major disadvantage on complex actions

---

## Gear and Equipment

### Tools and Equipment

**Improvised/Poor Tools**:
- -2 or disadvantage

**Standard Tools**:
- No modifier (or enables attempt)

**Quality Tools**:
- +2 or advantage

**Master-crafted Tools**:
- +4 or major advantage

---

### Weapons and Armor

**Weapon Quality**:
- Improvised: -2
- Standard: +0
- Fine: +1
- Masterwork: +2

**Armor Quality**:
- None: +0 defense
- Light: +1 defense
- Medium: +2 defense
- Heavy: +3 defense but mobility penalty

---

## Time Pressure

**Ample Time**:
- +2 or advantage
- Can retry failed checks
- Auto-success on easy tasks

**Standard Time**:
- No modifier

**Rushed**:
- -2 or disadvantage
- Risk of mistakes

**Immediate/Panic**:
- Major disadvantage
- Possible auto-failure on complex tasks

---

## Assistance and Teamwork

### Direct Assistance
**One ally helps**:
- Advantage on check OR +2

**Multiple helpers**:
- Still just advantage (doesn't stack)

**Expert helper**:
- Advantage plus maybe +1

---

### Group Checks
**Everyone rolls**:
- Success if majority succeed
- OR each faces consequence individually

---

### Combined Effort
**Multiple PCs working together**:
- One PC leads, rolls with advantage
- Others provide bonuses: +1 each (max +3)

---

## Proficiency Examples

### Common Proficiencies
- Specific weapon: +1 with that weapon
- Specific tool: +1 with that tool
- Specific knowledge: +1 or advantage on related lore
- Specific language: Enables communication

---

## Stacking Examples

### Example 1: Climbing
**Base**: 2d6
**Athletics**: +2
**Climbing gear**: +1 (proficiency)
**Roped by ally**: +2 (situational)
**Total**: 2d6 +5

---

### Example 2: Persuasion
**Base**: 2d6
**Persuasion**: +3
**Noble background**: +1 (proficiency)
**Target is friendly**: Advantage
**Total**: 2d6 +4 with advantage

---

### Example 3: Combat
**Base**: 2d6
**Melee Combat**: +2
**Sword proficiency**: +1
**High ground**: Advantage
**Total**: 2d6 +3 with advantage

## Quick Modifier Decision Tree

**Step 1**: What skill applies?
- Add skill bonus

**Step 2**: Do any proficiencies apply?
- Add +1 or +2 from best proficiency

**Step 3**: What are the conditions?
- Good: +2 or advantage
- Bad: -2 or disadvantage
- Neutral: no change

**Step 4**: Calculate total
- Roll 2d6 + all bonuses
- Apply advantage/disadvantage
- Compare to DC

---

## Maximum Modifiers

### Practical Limits
**Skill bonuses**: Usually +0 to +5
**Proficiency bonuses**: Usually +1 to +2
**Situational modifiers**: Usually -4 to +4
**Total typical maximum**: Around +10

**Anything above +10 total is exceptional**

---

## Common Mistakes

### Don't
- Stack advantage (only one applies)
- Stack proficiencies (pick best one)
- Apply skill bonus twice
- Forget situational modifiers

### Do
- Add all different types of bonuses
- Apply advantage separately
- Consider conditions
- Use common sense

---

## Genre-Specific Modifiers

### Cozy
- Smaller modifiers overall (-2 to +2 typical)
- Emphasis on advantage/disadvantage
- Community help provides bonuses

### Fantasy
- Standard modifier system
- Magic provides variety of bonuses
- Legendary items give significant bonuses

### Horror/Mystery
- Emphasis on penalties and disadvantage
- Few bonuses available
- Conditions impactful

### Modern
- Technology provides bonuses
- Situational modifiers important
- Professional gear matters

### Sci-Fi
- Advanced tech provides major bonuses
- Ship systems stack differently
- AI assistance common

---

## Quick Reference Summary

**Typical Check**:
```
2d6
+ Skill bonus (0 to +5)
+ Proficiency bonus (+1 or +2 if applicable)
+ Situational modifier (-4 to +4)
with Advantage or Disadvantage (if applicable)
vs DC (6 to 18)
```

**Result**:
- 10+: Success
- 7-9: Success with cost
- 6-: Failure but story continues
