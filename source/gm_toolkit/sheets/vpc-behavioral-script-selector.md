# VPC Behavioral Script Selector

Quick reference for VPC (Vital Player Character) behavior and roleplay.

---

## What Are Behavioral Scripts?

**Behavioral scripts** are quick patterns for playing NPCs consistently without detailed prep.

**Use when**:
- Running NPC in combat
- Deciding NPC reactions
- Staying consistent with NPC personality
- Improvising NPC behavior

---

## Core Behavioral Dimensions

### Axis 1: Proactive ↔ Reactive
**Proactive**: Takes initiative, creates situations  
**Reactive**: Responds to situations, waits

### Axis 2: Aggressive ↔ Cautious
**Aggressive**: Risks everything, bold actions  
**Cautious**: Careful, measured, self-protective

### Axis 3: Loyal ↔ Self-Interested
**Loyal**: Serves cause/person above self  
**Self-Interested**: Prioritizes own benefit

---

## Quick Behavioral Scripts

### Script 1: The Protector
**Core Drive**: Defend ward/group/ideal

**In Combat**:
- Position between threat and ward
- Take hits meant for others
- Focus threats to ward
- Ignore personal danger

**In Social**:
- Speak up for ward
- Deflect criticism
- Watch for threats
- Sacrifice own interests

**When Stressed**: Become more aggressive in defense

---

### Script 2: The Schemer
**Core Drive**: Advance personal agenda

**In Combat**:
- Fight smart, not hard
- Flee if disadvantaged
- Use others as shields
- Strike when opportune

**In Social**:
- Manipulate conversations
- Gather information
- Plant seeds for later
- Build useful relationships

**When Stressed**: Betray if necessary

---

### Script 3: The Zealot
**Core Drive**: Serve cause absolutely

**In Combat**:
- No retreat, no surrender
- Sacrifice self for cause
- Convert or destroy
- Ignore pain/danger

**In Social**:
- Proselytize constantly
- See world through cause
- Condemn opposition
- Inspire/pressure others

**When Stressed**: Double down on beliefs

---

### Script 4: The Professional
**Core Drive**: Complete the job

**In Combat**:
- Efficient, no showboating
- Assess and adapt
- Retreat if job impossible
- Kill quickly, move on

**In Social**:
- Businesslike, brief
- Honor contracts
- Clear about terms
- No personal investment

**When Stressed**: Focus narrows to job completion

---

### Script 5: The Survivor
**Core Drive**: Stay alive

**In Combat**:
- Flee when losing
- Fight dirty if cornered
- Use environment
- No honor, only survival

**In Social**:
- Say what keeps you safe
- Betray if necessary
- Read danger carefully
- Keep escape routes

**When Stressed**: Panic and flee or desperate violence

---

### Script 6: The Idealist
**Core Drive**: Do what's right

**In Combat**:
- Fight honorably
- Protect innocents
- Offer mercy
- Hesitate at cruelty

**In Social**:
- Speak truth
- Champion justice
- Trust too easily
- Inspire others

**When Stressed**: Doubt self or become rigid

---

### Script 7: The Pragmatist
**Core Drive**: Best outcome for most people

**In Combat**:
- Assess cost/benefit
- Accept necessary casualties
- Retreat if too costly
- End fights efficiently

**In Social**:
- Negotiate constantly
- Find compromises
- Calculate outcomes
- Build coalitions

**When Stressed**: Make hard choices coldly

---

### Script 8: The Chaos Agent
**Core Drive**: Disrupt and destabilize

**In Combat**:
- Unpredictable tactics
- Create confusion
- No clear allegiance
- Maximum disruption

**In Social**:
- Stir conflict
- Reveal secrets
- Undermine authority
- Enjoy watching collapse

**When Stressed**: Escalate chaos

---

### Script 9: The Follower
**Core Drive**: Serve leader/group

**In Combat**:
- Follow orders exactly
- Look to leader
- Protect leadership
- No independent action

**In Social**:
- Echo leader
- Defer to group
- Avoid decisions
- Need direction

**When Stressed**: Panic without leadership

---

### Script 10: The Mentor
**Core Drive**: Guide and teach

**In Combat**:
- Call out advice
- Protect students
- Teach through action
- Hold back power

**In Social**:
- Offer wisdom
- Ask leading questions
- Create learning moments
- Patience with mistakes

**When Stressed**: Disappointed in students

## Combining Scripts

**NPCs can have primary and secondary scripts**:

**Example 1**: Protector (primary) + Survivor (secondary)
- Will defend ward unless clearly suicidal
- Retreat with ward if possible
- Personal survival secondary to ward

**Example 2**: Professional (primary) + Idealist (secondary)
- Does job well but has moral lines
- Won't take evil contracts
- Conflict between duty and morality

**Example 3**: Zealot (primary) + Schemer (secondary)
- Serves cause through manipulation
- Ends justify means
- Patient long-term planning

## Script Modifiers

### Under Pressure
- **Protector**: More aggressive
- **Schemer**: More desperate/reckless
- **Zealot**: More extreme
- **Professional**: More focused
- **Survivor**: Flight or fight response
- **Idealist**: Doubt or rigidity
- **Pragmatist**: Harder choices
- **Chaos Agent**: More chaotic
- **Follower**: Panic
- **Mentor**: Disappointment/protective

### When Winning
- **Protector**: Relax vigilance
- **Schemer**: Press advantage
- **Zealot**: Push harder
- **Professional**: Efficient completion
- **Survivor**: Secure position
- **Idealist**: Offer mercy
- **Pragmatist**: Lock in victory
- **Chaos Agent**: Create more chaos
- **Follower**: Look for orders
- **Mentor**: Teach lesson

### When Losing
- **Protector**: Last stand
- **Schemer**: Betray/flee
- **Zealot**: Martyrdom
- **Professional**: Retreat tactically
- **Survivor**: Panic flee
- **Idealist**: Question self
- **Pragmatist**: Cut losses
- **Chaos Agent**: Burn it all down
- **Follower**: Collapse
- **Mentor**: Cover student retreat

## Quick NPC Decision Tool

**When NPC must decide, ask**:

1. **What does this script prioritize?**
2. **What would this script avoid?**
3. **How does this script handle stress?**

**Then pick the option that fits the script.**

- Create barriers

- Flee when hurt

- Offer surrender
- Avoid killing

## Adaptation and Growth

### When Script Should Change

**Loyalty broken**:
- Protector → Survivor or Schemer
- Follower → Survivor or Independent
- Idealist → Pragmatist or Zealot

**Beliefs challenged**:
- Zealot → Idealist or Schemer
- Idealist → Pragmatist
- Follower → Independent

**Near-death experience**:
- Professional → Survivor
- Idealist → Pragmatist
- Brave → Cautious
