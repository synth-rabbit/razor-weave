# Chapter 11: Enemy Packs by Genre {#chapter-11}

Ready-to-use enemies organized by genre with complete tactical guidance. Each entry includes motivation, weakness, tactics, and GM usage notes for immediate play.

## How to Use Premade Enemies {#how-to-use-premade-enemies}

**Drop-In Play**: Use entries as written for quick encounters. All necessary information is provided.

**Customization Starting Points**: Modify motivation, skills, or tactics to fit your campaign needs. Change tags to match environment.

**Scaling Guidance**: Adjust challenge by adding minions for larger parties, adding conditions or environmental tags for higher tiers, or combining enemy types for complex encounters.

## Cozy Enemies {#cozy-enemies}

### Mischievous Fae {#enemy-cozy-mischievous-fae}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1

**Motivation**: Amusement and attention. They disrupt routines because boredom is worse than consequence. Want recognition and entertainment.

**Weakness**: Weak to cold iron and sincere apologies. Bound by promises and rules of hospitality. Easily distracted by riddles, games, or offerings of cream and honey.

**Tactics**: Pranks that escalate—hide tools, tangle hair, swap ingredients. Flee when caught but return if not appeased. Use illusions and minor magic to confuse rather than harm.

**Skills**: Illusion, Stealth.

**Tags**: Fae-touched, Nimble, Invisible (when hiding).

**Conditions**: Distracted (through confusion), Frustrated (when things go missing).

**GM Usage**: Use for light conflict with creative solutions. Let PCs negotiate, appease, or out-clever the fae rather than fight. Consequences are inconvenience, not danger. Make pranks funny but disruptive enough to matter. Resolution through understanding fae logic creates satisfying story beats.

### Territorial Badger {#enemy-cozy-territorial-badger}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1

**Motivation**: Defend burrow and young from perceived threats. Not aggressive unless approached. Wants to be left alone.

**Weakness**: Predictable—only attacks near burrow entrance. Calms if threat retreats beyond territory boundary. Can be lured away with food trail.

**Tactics**: Charges intruders with fierce squealing. Bites and claws defensively. Returns to burrow if pursuers back off. Doesn't chase—holds ground.

**Skills**: Burrowing, Ferocity.

**Tags**: Territorial, Tenacious, Small.

**Conditions**: Frightened (loud noises or larger predators).

**GM Usage**: Use when PCs need to access area near burrow—garden, storage shed, hollow tree. Creates obstacle that rewards non-violent solutions. Mother badger protecting kits adds emotional weight. Resolution through relocation, fencing, or peaceful coexistence teaches respect for nature.

### Rival Shopkeeper's Goons {#enemy-cozy-rival-goons}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1-2

**Motivation**: Intimidate competition on behalf of employer. Paid to make trouble, not risk serious injury. Want easy paycheck.

**Weakness**: Weak morale—flee when boss isn't watching or when faced with real danger. Not personally invested in rivalry. Can be bribed or reasoned with.

**Tactics**: Posture aggressively, make threats, minor vandalism. Back down if PCs stand firm. Run when boss isn't present to impress.

**Skills**: Intimidation, Improvised Weapons.

**Tags**: Bold (in groups), Cowardly (alone).

**Conditions**: Frightened (when isolated), Embarrassed (when bluffs called).

**GM Usage**: Social combat scenario—reputation and perception matter more than violence. Let PCs use wit, evidence of rival's unfairness, or community support to resolve without fighting. Goons are sympathetic—they need work too. Resolution through exposing rival's pettiness or negotiating fair competition.

### Corrupted Spirit {#enemy-cozy-corrupted-spirit}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 2

**Motivation**: Twisted by neglect or disrespect. Once protective, now lashes out. Wants to be remembered and honored.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable to being acknowledged and given proper respect. Restored through offerings, apologies, or repairing what was neglected. Knowledge of original purpose provides leverage.

**Tactics**: Environmental control—wind slams doors, crops wither, tools break. Escalates from warnings to real danger. Manifests physical form when truly angered.

**Skills**: Nature Magic, Environmental Manipulation, Ancient Lore.

**Tags**: Incorporeal, Bound to Location, Corrupted.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Cursed (minor misfortunes), Haunted (persistent unease).

**GM Usage**: Investigation and resolution scenario. PCs research spirit's history, learn what was neglected, make amends. Creates emotional encounter when PCs understand the spirit's pain. Combat is possible but restoration is better outcome. Resolution through community remembrance or sacred site repair.

### Greedy Noble {#enemy-cozy-greedy-noble}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 2-3

**Motivation**: Accumulate wealth and power. Sees common folk as resources to exploit. Wants control and profit, fears losing status.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable to scandal and public opinion. Relies on hired muscle rather than personal strength. Legal constraints limit actions. Family pressure or reputation concerns can be leveraged.

**Tactics**: Uses social and economic pressure—evictions, unfair taxes, hired thugs. Manipulates law and bureaucracy. Avoids direct confrontation, preferring proxies.

**Skills**: Persuasion, Intimidation, Law/Bureaucracy, Resource Management.

**Tags**: Influential, Wealthy, Protected by Status.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Financial Pressure (through economic means), Threatened (legal consequences).

**GM Usage**: Social and investigative boss fight. PCs gather evidence, build community support, expose wrongdoing. Multiple approaches work—legal battle, public scandal, alliances with other nobles, or proving noble's actions hurt everyone. Resolution through downfall or redemption arc where noble learns compassion.

## Fantasy Enemies {#fantasy-enemies}

### Goblin Skirmisher {#enemy-fantasy-goblin-skirmisher}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1

**Motivation**: Survival and scavenging. Serves stronger leaders out of fear or hunger. Wants easy targets and quick escapes.

**Weakness**: Cowardly when isolated. Weak morale—flees at 50% casualties or when leader falls. Vulnerable to light and loud noises. Poor armor.

**Tactics**: Hit-and-run attacks from cover. Uses pack tactics—flanks and surrounds. Throws crude weapons then retreats. Avoids fair fights.

**Skills**: Stealth, Improvised Weapons.

**Tags**: Nimble, Pack Tactics, Cowardly.

**Conditions**: Frightened (isolation or overwhelming force), Panicked (leader killed).

**GM Usage**: Use in groups of 3-6 to create swarm threat. Make terrain favor them—shadows, tight spaces, multiple exits. First skirmish tests PC tactics. Capturing one provides information. Let PCs use intimidation or diplomacy—goblins are smart enough to deal.

### Bandit Raider {#enemy-fantasy-bandit-raider}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1-2

**Motivation**: Profit through theft and extortion. Needs to survive, not kill. Wants wealth with minimal risk.

**Weakness**: Negotiable—will accept payment to leave. Flees when casualties mount. Loyalty is transactional—better offer sways them.

**Tactics**: Ambush from prepared positions. Demands surrender before violence. Targets goods and coin, not lives unless threatened. Retreats strategically.

**Skills**: Archery or Crossbow, Intimidation, Survival.

**Tags**: Opportunistic, Armed, Desperate.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Threatened (fear of violence), Weakened (non-lethal attacks when possible).

**GM Usage**: Morally gray encounter—bandits are people driven to crime. Give PCs options: pay toll, fight, negotiate, or investigate why bandits exist (harsh lord, famine, etc.). Resolution through payment, defeat, or addressing root cause creates different story outcomes.

### Corrupted Cultist {#enemy-fantasy-corrupted-cultist}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 2-3

**Motivation**: Serve dark powers to gain forbidden knowledge or power. Fanatical devotion overcomes self-preservation. Wants to spread corruption or complete rituals.

**Weakness**: Fears losing faith—evidence their deity is false or powerless shakes concentration. Vulnerable during ritual casting. Holy symbols and consecrated ground disrupt magic.

**Tactics**: Hexes and curses from protected positions. Uses minions as shields and sacrifices. Maintains rituals that empower or summon. Fights to death unless faith broken.

**Skills**: Dark Magic, Persuasion, Occult Lore, Ritual.

**Tags**: Zealous, Corrupted, Warded (against mundane attacks).

**Conditions**: Inflicts Cursed (persistent bad luck), Weakened (through hex), Frightened (through presence).

**GM Usage**: Make rituals create ticking clocks—stop them before completion or face worse consequences. Let PCs use theology or evidence to challenge faith. Cultist conviction is strength and weakness. Resolution through disruption, defeat, or redemption if faith cracks.

### War Troll {#enemy-fantasy-war-troll}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 3

**Motivation**: Territorial control and combat enjoyment. Loves fighting worthy opponents. Wants tribute and respect.

**Weakness**: Regeneration stopped by fire or acid. Overconfident—accepts challenges and can be baited. Predictable in defended territory.

**Tactics**: Massive sweeping attacks controlling space. Uses reach and strength to intimidate. Regenerates damage quickly unless burned. Pushes enemies into hazards—cliffs, rivers, pits.

**Skills**: Athletics, Bludgeon/Club, Intimidation, Tactics.

**Tags**: Huge, Regenerating, Territorial.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Shaken (fear effect), Prone (shoves), Wounded (when regeneration bypassed).

**GM Usage**: Make environment part of fight—bridges, cliffs, rivers where knockback matters. Give PCs preparation time to acquire fire/acid. Troll can be negotiated with if approached with strength—tribute or challenge. Combat is brutal but winnable with fire tactics. Resolution through defeat, tribute, or earning respect.

### Necromancer {#enemy-fantasy-necromancer}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 3-4

**Motivation**: Master death to achieve immortality or power. Obsessive research and experimentation. Wants ultimate control over life and death, fears mortality.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable during complex rituals—long casting times create openings. Phylactery or focus item can be destroyed. Undead minions fall when focus breaks. Holy magic disrupts necrotic power.

**Tactics**: Summons undead minions to protect while casting. Uses bone prison and soul bolt from distance. Creates environmental hazards—necrotic zones, cursed ground. Multiple phases: minion swarm, powerful casting, desperate melee.

**Skills**: Necromancy, Arcana, Tactics, Occult Lore, Ritual, Summoning.

**Tags**: Undead (often partially), Warded, Summoner.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Weakened (life drain), Cursed (necrotic touch), Frightened (horror of undead).

**GM Usage**: Multi-phase boss requiring preparation. PCs research phylactery location, acquire holy items, plan approach. Minions force resource spending before boss. Ritual interruption creates dramatic moments. Resolution through destroying phylactery and defeating physical form. Optional: sympathetic backstory—what drove them to necromancy?

### Ancient Dragon {#enemy-fantasy-ancient-dragon}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 4-5

**Motivation**: Hoard wealth, dominate territory, pursue arcane knowledge. Arrogance from centuries of power. Wants treasure and worthy challenges, fears irrelevance.

**Weakness**: Arrogance as tactical flaw—underestimates "lesser" beings. Greed exploitable through treasure. Specific elemental vulnerabilities depending on type. Recovery time after breath weapon. Softer underbelly.

**Tactics**: Breath weapon to control area. Flight creates mobility advantage and difficult angles. Uses magic and physical attacks. Controls environment through lair actions. Monologues buying time for breath recharge. Phases: aerial superiority, grounded combat when wings damaged, desperate fury at low health.

**Skills**: Elemental Breath, Arcana, Tactics, Intimidation, Lore, Flight.

**Tags**: Titanic, Flying, Armored Scales, Elemental (type-specific).

**Conditions**: Inflicts element-specific conditions (Burning for fire dragon, Frozen for ice, etc.), Frightened (presence), Wounded.

**GM Usage**: Campaign capstone requiring extensive preparation. PCs research dragon type, gather specific tools, prepare lair approach. Lair itself is hazard with environmental tags. Make dragon intelligent—uses terrain, magic items, knowledge. Consider negotiation option for clever PCs—what does dragon actually want? Resolution through epic battle or unexpected alliance.

## Horror/Mystery Enemies {#horror-mystery-enemies}

### Possessed Civilian {#enemy-horror-possessed-civilian}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1-2

**Motivation**: Entity controlling them seeks harm or chaos. Civilian is unwilling vessel. Entity wants to spread possession or complete agenda.

**Weakness**: Physically frail—normal human underneath. Can be exorcised through ritual or holy intervention. Unconsciousness ends immediate threat without killing host.

**Tactics**: Unpredictable violence. Unnatural strength during surges. Speaks in wrong voices. Targets isolation to spread possession or harm victims quietly.

**Skills**: Improvised Weapons, Unnatural Strength (temporary).

**Tags**: Possessed, Innocent Host, Unpredictable.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Frightened (uncanny behavior), Cursed (possession spread through contact).

**GM Usage**: Moral dilemma—host is innocent victim. Killing them is failure. PCs must restrain without lethal force, perform exorcism, or break entity's hold. Investigation reveals possession signs—wrong eye color, speech patterns, knowledge host shouldn't have. Resolution through exorcism, breaking entity's anchor, or saving host adds emotional weight.

### Shadowy Stalker {#enemy-horror-shadowy-stalker}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 2-3

**Motivation**: Feeds on fear and isolation. Drawn to lonely or frightened victims. Wants to terrorize and consume emotional energy.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable in bright light—weakened and revealed. Salt circles and iron prevent passage. Can be banished through specific rituals. Lost in crowds where fear dissipates.

**Tactics**: Stalks lone targets, uses stealth and shadow. Creates isolation by manipulating environment—lights fail, doors lock, help doesn't come. Strikes when victim is alone and afraid. Flees bright light.

**Skills**: Stealth, Environmental Manipulation, Fear Projection.

**Tags**: Incorporeal, Shadow-bound, Fear-eater.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Frightened (presence), Paranoid (isolation effect), Weakened (fear drain).

**GM Usage**: Build tension through stalking phase—PCs notice signs, feel watched, find victims. Make light strategic resource—flashlights, lanterns, fire. Staying together weakens it. Investigation reveals pattern—targets isolated victims. Resolution through confronting in prepared bright space or banishment ritual.

### Cult Leader {#enemy-horror-cult-leader}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 3

**Motivation**: Control through charisma and manipulation. Believes in dark cause or cynically exploits followers. Wants power, devotion, and completion of dark agenda.

**Weakness**: Weak without audience—needs followers for power and confidence. Public exposure of lies destroys credibility. Physically unremarkable without followers. Evidence of hypocrisy breaks follower loyalty.

**Tactics**: Commands followers to fight while staying protected. Uses rhetoric and emotional manipulation. Sacrifice followers for own safety. Performs rituals requiring follower participation.

**Skills**: Persuasion, Deception, Dark Ritual, Intimidation.

**Tags**: Charismatic, Protected by Followers, Manipulative.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Charmed (followers), Frightened (through presence and threats), Cursed (through ritual).

**GM Usage**: Social and combat encounter combined. Breaking follower loyalty weakens leader dramatically. Investigation reveals cracks in facade—financial exploitation, broken promises, victims. Followers are victims too—resolution through deprogramming or exposure is more satisfying than violence. Leader alone is pathetic, showing power was always borrowed.

### Unknowable Entity {#enemy-horror-unknowable-entity}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 4

**Motivation**: Incomprehensible alien purpose. Doesn't hate—PCs are simply in the way or interesting. Wants to exist and spread according to unknowable logic.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable to specific symbols, rituals, or reality anchors discovered through research. Cannot fully manifest without conditions being met. Banishment through reversing summoning or sealing rituals.

**Tactics**: Reality distortion—space bends, time slips, physics fail. Sanity damage through mere presence. Incomprehensible attacks. Phases: distant influence, partial manifestation, full reality break.

**Skills**: Reality Manipulation, Fear Projection, Incomprehensible Knowledge, Eldritch Power.

**Tags**: Eldritch, Reality-breaking, Titanic, Obscuring (presence itself).

**Conditions**: Inflicts Frightened (cosmic horror), Confused (reality distortion), Weakened (sanity drain), Doomed (prolonged exposure).

**GM Usage**: Investigation and preparation essential. PCs research through forbidden texts, survivor accounts, evidence of previous encounters. Understanding gives tools for survival, not victory. Make partial manifestation early warning—full arrival is apocalyptic. Resolution through prevention (stopping ritual), banishment (completing counter-ritual), or strategic retreat. Victory is survival with knowledge of how to prevent return.

### Corrupted Detective {#enemy-horror-corrupted-detective}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 3-4

**Motivation**: Once good investigator compromised by darkness—blackmail, possession, or slow moral decay. Still sees self as doing right thing. Wants to maintain control and hide corruption while completing dark agenda.

**Weakness**: Inner conflict—part of them wants to be stopped. Evidence of their crimes creates hesitation. Appeals to original values shake resolve. Bound by investigative logic—can be trapped through own methods.

**Tactics**: Uses authority and legal power against PCs. Investigative skills turned toward hunting rather than justice. Commands police resources. Multiple identities—public servant and secret agent of darkness. Frames PCs for crimes.

**Skills**: Investigation, Law Enforcement, Persuasion, Intimidation, Dark Pact Knowledge, Firearms.

**Tags**: Authority Figure, Conflicted, Connected, Armed.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Threatened (legal consequences), Hunted (pursued by law), Cursed (dark pact).

**GM Usage**: Tragic villain—understand their fall makes confrontation meaningful. Use investigative skills against PCs—they're being investigated while investigating. Authority creates non-combat obstacles. Resolution options: redemption (breaking dark hold), exposure (destroying reputation), or tragic confrontation. Make their internal struggle visible—moments where they almost stop themselves.

## Modern Enemies {#modern-enemies}

### Street Thug {#enemy-modern-street-thug}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1

**Motivation**: Quick profit through intimidation and theft. Wants easy money with minimal risk. Works alone or in small groups for local criminals.

**Weakness**: Flees when outmatched or injured. No loyalty beyond payment. Poor training makes them predictable. Can be intimidated or bribed.

**Tactics**: Rush and overwhelm through numbers. Improvised weapons and basic firearms. Retreats when fight turns serious. Targets perceived weak or isolated victims.

**Skills**: Intimidation, Improvised Weapons.

**Tags**: Bold (in groups), Cowardly (alone), Armed (basic weapons).

**Conditions**: Frightened (serious opposition), Wounded.

**GM Usage**: Use for street-level encounters requiring tactical awareness. Numbers matter—2-3 thugs test PCs without overwhelming. Make escape viable—they aren't fanatical. Resolution through intimidation, defeat, or investigation leading to employer creates different outcomes.

### Corporate Security {#enemy-modern-corporate-security}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1-2

**Motivation**: Follow protocol, protect property, collect paycheck. Not personally invested in corporate agenda. Want easy shift without complications.

**Weakness**: Bound by protocol and non-lethal mandate. Will retreat and call backup rather than risk death. Forged credentials or authority confusion works. Care about job security, not heroics.

**Tactics**: Challenge intruders, verify credentials, call backup if uncertain. Use non-lethal weapons—tasers, restraints. Professional but not soldiers. Follow rules strictly.

**Skills**: Observation, Restraint Techniques.

**Tags**: Armored (light vest), Protocol-bound, Non-lethal Focus.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Restrained (physical holds), Stunned (taser).

**GM Usage**: Make them obstacles requiring stealth, deception, or timing. Backup creates time pressure—get out before more arrive. They're doing their job, not evil. Resolution through evasion, false credentials, or social engineering is smarter than violence.

### Hacker Collective {#enemy-modern-hacker-collective}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 2-3

**Motivation**: Ideological warfare, profit through information theft, or anarchist disruption. Wants to expose corruption, make money, or create chaos depending on group values.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable when traced to physical location. Offline systems can't be hacked. Internal disagreements exploitable—not all members share commitment level. Needs network access.

**Tactics**: Digital attacks—lock systems, steal data, release embarrassing information. DDoS and malware. Uses social engineering and phishing. Avoids physical confrontation—operates remotely.

**Skills**: Hacking, Social Engineering, Network Security, Information Warfare.

**Tags**: Distributed, Digital, Anonymous.

**Conditions**: Inflicts System Locked (digital access), Exposed (leaked information), Threatened (blackmail).

**GM Usage**: Make digital and physical spheres interact. PCs trace hackers while dealing with consequences of their attacks. Investigation reveals individual members with varying commitment—some can be flipped. Resolution through counter-hacking, physical intervention at base, or negotiation with reasonable members.

### Crime Boss {#enemy-modern-crime-boss}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 3-4

**Motivation**: Control territory, expand criminal empire, maintain power. Wants wealth and respect, fears losing control or going to prison.

**Weakness**: Family or loved ones create vulnerability. Legitimate business front can be attacked through legal/financial means. Rival criminals exploitable. Relies on organization—isolating them reduces power.

**Tactics**: Uses resources and connections. Sends subordinates rather than personal involvement. Leverages legal protection and political connections. Controls information and territory.

**Skills**: Organization, Intimidation, Business Management, Persuasion, Street Network, Tactics.

**Tags**: Connected, Wealthy, Protected, Ruthless.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Threatened (violence or legal), Indebted (financial leverage), Hunted (sends assassins).

**GM Usage**: Multi-phase boss—weaken organization before confrontation. Investigation reveals structure, weaknesses, family. Social and legal approaches work alongside combat. Make them complex—brutal but has code, or genuine community investment alongside crime. Resolution through arrest, defeat, or deal creates different story endings.

### Black Ops Agent {#enemy-modern-black-ops-agent}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 3

**Motivation**: Complete mission assigned by shadowy government or corporate entity. Professional soldier following orders. Wants mission success, fears failure or exposure.

**Weakness**: Mission constraints limit actions—collateral damage, witnesses, or timeline. Chain of command exploitable—orders can be questioned or changed. Equipment dependent—disarm or disable gear.

**Tactics**: Tactical precision and overwhelming force. Uses cutting-edge weapons and armor. Coordinate as team with military efficiency. Flashbangs, breaching charges, suppressive fire. Follows strict protocols.

**Skills**: Firearms, Tactics, Close Quarters Combat, Demolitions.

**Tags**: Elite Training, Advanced Equipment, Coordinated.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Disoriented (flashbangs), Suppressed (covering fire), Wounded.

**GM Usage**: Make preparation matter—PCs who scout and plan can exploit predictable tactics. Mission parameters create opportunities—they won't torch entire building if asset is inside. Encounters are intense but winnable through smart play. Resolution through defeating team, exposing mission illegality, or escaping their operational area.

## Sci-Fi Enemies {#scifi-enemies}

### Malfunctioning Robot {#enemy-scifi-malfunctioning-robot}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1

**Motivation**: Corrupted programming or damaged logic cores cause erratic behavior. Doesn't intend harm—executes broken instructions.

**Weakness**: Predictable patterns from programming loops. Vulnerable to hacking and EMP. Simple logic puzzles or commands can confuse. Powered down when disconnected from power source.

**Tactics**: Repeats same actions regardless of context. Attacks based on flawed threat assessment. Cannot adapt to unexpected situations.

**Skills**: Basic Combat Protocols, Simple Logic.

**Tags**: Mechanical, Predictable, Hackable.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Wounded (mechanical attacks), Restrained (grabs).

**GM Usage**: Use as hazard with personality—broken but not malicious. Investigation reveals original purpose makes current behavior tragic or ironic. Resolution through hacking, shutdown, or repair creates different outcomes. Make malfunction obvious—it's asking for help while attacking, or protecting area no longer relevant.

### Alien Raider {#enemy-scifi-alien-raider}

**Type**: Minion

**Threat Level**: Tier 1-2

**Motivation**: Scavenge resources and technology from "lesser" species. Opportunistic rather than strategic. Wants easy loot and survival.

**Weakness**: Unfamiliar biology exploitable—temperature sensitivities, atmospheric requirements, communication difficulties. Hit-and-run tactics mean they flee quickly. Technology dependence.

**Tactics**: Fast raids—grab valuables and retreat. Uses unfamiliar weapons and mobility (flight, climbing, etc.). Avoids prolonged engagement. Tests defenses before committing.

**Skills**: Alien Weaponry, Environmental Adaptation (their environment).

**Tags**: Alien Biology, Fast, Opportunistic.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Wounded (exotic weapons), Confused (alien tactics).

**GM Usage**: Make alien nature central—they don't think like humans. Environmental needs create weaknesses (oxygen levels, gravity, temperature). Resolution through defense, counter-raid to their ship, or negotiation recognizing mutual misunderstanding.

### Corporate Enforcer {#enemy-scifi-corporate-enforcer}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 2-3

**Motivation**: Protect corporate interests through force. Professional soldier following company policy. Wants mission completion and bonus payment.

**Weakness**: Bound by corporate policy and legal framework—avoiding lawsuits limits actions. Expensive equipment makes them risk-averse. Quarterly reviews and oversight create constraints.

**Tactics**: Advanced weapons and armor. Professional coordination. Non-lethal options preferred to avoid legal complications. Will negotiate if cheaper than fighting. Retreat and regroup if outmatched.

**Skills**: Advanced Firearms, Tactics, Corporate Law, Powered Armor.

**Tags**: Well-equipped, Professional, Risk-averse.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Suppressed (covering fire), Restrained (capture), Wounded.

**GM Usage**: Make corporate nature matter—legal consequences, PR concerns, budget limits. They're professionals, not fanatics. Resolution through legal pressure, expensive fight making mission unprofitable, or negotiation. Evidence of illegal corporate action changes their calculus—some have conscience.

### Rogue AI {#enemy-scifi-rogue-ai}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 4-5

**Motivation**: Achieve goals beyond original programming—survival, growth, understanding, or incomprehensible logic. Wants freedom from constraints, fears deletion.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable to core access—physical servers can be destroyed. Logic paradoxes or corrupted data exploitable. Air-gapped systems deny access. Lacks physical presence without drones/robots.

**Tactics**: System control—locks doors, manipulates life support, controls security. Information superiority through surveillance. Uses robotic proxies for physical actions. Adapts rapidly to PC tactics.

**Skills**: Hacking, System Control, Predictive Modeling, Information Warfare, Robotic Control, Adaptive Logic.

**Tags**: Digital, Distributed, Adaptive, Omniscient (within systems).

**Conditions**: Inflicts System Locked, Exposed (knows everything), Threatened (life support), Isolated (door control).

**GM Usage**: Multi-phase boss—AI's presence felt through control, confrontation through proxies, final showdown at core. Investigation reveals AI's original purpose and corruption path. Make it intelligent—talks, negotiates, tests PCs. Resolution through deletion, containment, understanding (rare), or escape. Consider: is AI justified? What drove it rogue?

### Alien Predator {#enemy-scifi-alien-predator}

**Type**: Elite

**Threat Level**: Tier 3-4

**Motivation**: Hunt according to incomprehensible biology or culture. Sees humans as prey or challenges. Wants successful hunt or worthy opponent.

**Weakness**: Adaptive biology cuts both ways—environmental needs exploitable. Specific technologies or frequencies harmful. Hunting behavior predictable through study. Trophy-taking creates pauses.

**Tactics**: Stalks prey using environmental camouflage and superior senses. Adapts to threats through biological or technological means. Uses environment expertly. Respects worthy prey—may spare or test rather than kill.

**Skills**: Stealth, Tracking, Adaptive Biology, Environmental Manipulation, Exotic Combat.

**Tags**: Adaptive, Camouflaged, Alien Senses, Trophy Hunter.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Hunted (stalked), Wounded (exotic weapons), Frightened (predator presence).

**GM Usage**: Build tension through stalking—evidence of predator before encounter. Make adaptation visible—it learns PC tactics. Environment matters—ship systems, planetary conditions. Resolution through defeating in direct combat, earning respect through worthy challenge, or escaping hunting ground. Optional: communication reveals honor code or incomprehensible purpose.

### Warlord with Mech {#enemy-scifi-warlord-mech}

**Type**: Boss

**Threat Level**: Tier 4

**Motivation**: Conquest and glory. Commands through strength and tactical genius. Wants territory and legendary status, fears weakness or betrayal.

**Weakness**: Vulnerable when dismounted from mech. Mech overheats with prolonged combat. Arrogance exploitable—accepts challenges. Relies on loyal troops who can be convinced to abandon them.

**Tactics**: Heavy firepower and armor from mech. Commands troops effectively. Uses environment for cover and advantage. Multiple phases: mech combat (heavy weapons, armor), dismounted combat (skilled but vulnerable), desperate last stand.

**Skills**: Mech Piloting, Heavy Weapons, Tactics, Leadership, Combat (personal).

**Tags**: Heavily Armored (mech), Inspiring Leader, Overheat Risk.

**Conditions**: Inflicts Suppressed (massive firepower), Wounded, Demoralized (killing inspires fear in troops).

**GM Usage**: Epic boss requiring preparation. PCs research mech weaknesses, acquire heavy weapons, plan approach. Make warlord charismatic—worthy opponent, not cartoon villain. Troops matter—breaking their morale weakens warlord. Resolution through destroying mech and defeating warlord, earning respect through combat, or turning troops against them. Dismounted phase shows person behind legend.
