# Chapter 43: NPC, VPC, and Enemy Quick Reference {#chapter-43}

This chapter consolidates all GM character creation tools into accessible checklists and time-boxed procedures for immediate use at the table.

## NPC Quick Creation {#npc-quick-creation}

Use time-boxed procedures to create NPCs with appropriate detail for their narrative importance.

### 5-Minute NPC {#npc-5-minute}

**When to Use**: Background characters, vendors, guards, witnesses, one-scene appearances

**Procedure**:
1. **Name**: Generate or pick appropriate to setting
2. **Role**: Choose oneâ€”Ally, Rival, Mentor, Antagonist, Informant (Chapter 38)
3. **One Trait**: Single distinctive personality feature
4. **One Goal**: What they want right now

**Example**: Captain Rusk (Informant). Overworked, practical. Wants help covering guard shortage.

**Usage**: Enough for consistent roleplay. Promote to 10-minute if NPC becomes important.

### 10-Minute NPC {#npc-10-minute}

**When to Use**: Multi-scene NPCs, quest-givers, recurring contacts, minor faction members

**Procedure**:
1. **Name and Role**: As above
2. **Personality** (2-3 traits): Traits that guide behavior
3. **Goal**: What they want and why it matters
4. **Secret**: One hidden fact or complication
5. **Skills** (2-3): Capabilities from Mechanics Reference

**Example**: Sister Morwen (Ally). Devout, practical, calm. Goal: protect community from occult threats. Secret: hears cursed artifact whispering. Skills: Ritual Knowledge, Composure, Occult Lore.

**Usage**: Run multiple encounters, create hooks, know competencies.

### 20-Minute NPC {#npc-20-minute}

**When to Use**: Major recurring NPCs, faction leaders, primary antagonists, long-term allies

**Procedure**:
1. **Complete Role Template** (Chapter 38): Full personality, goal, secret, skills, notable trait
2. **Relationships**: Connections to 1-2 other NPCs or factions
3. **Arc Potential**: How NPC might evolve based on PC actions
4. **Visual/Behavioral Details**: Appearance, mannerisms, speech patterns

**Example**: Archivist Lysa (Informant/Ally). Curious, absent-minded, protective. Goal: preserve dangerous knowledge. Secret: maintains hidden ledger of banned books. Skills: Research, History, Appraisal. Relationships: reports to Head Librarian (antagonist), helps young scholar. Arc: becomes ally if PCs prove trustworthy, antagonist if knowledge abused. Details: ink-stained fingers, adjusts spectacles, tucks hair when lying, office smells of mint tea.

**Usage**: Campaign-spanning NPC with dynamic relationships.

### NPC Role Guide {#npc-role-guide}

**Ally** (Chapter 38): Supports PCs; provides resources, information, or assistance. Has own goals and limits.

**Rival** (Chapter 38): Competes with PCs for same goals. Creates tension without hostility. Can become ally or enemy.

**Mentor** (Chapter 38): Teaches and guides PCs. Has knowledge to share and limitations preventing direct action.

**Antagonist** (Chapter 38): Opposes PCs with clear goals. Creates conflict through competing interests. Has exploitable weaknesses.

**Informant** (Chapter 38): Provides information transactionally. Knows valuable secrets, wants something in return.

## VPC Quick Creation {#vpc-quick-creation}

Create Virtual Player Characters that support without overshadowing.

### VPC Creation Steps {#vpc-creation-steps}

**Step 1: Identify Party Gap**
- What capability does the party lack? (healing, combat, social, technical)
- What role would support without stealing spotlight?

**Step 2: Choose Behavioral Script** (Chapter 40)
- **Loyal Protector**: Defensive, prioritizes PC safety, warns of danger
- **Curious Explorer**: Asks questions, investigates details, provides observations
- **Technical Expert**: Handles specialty tasks, defers on other decisions
- **Moral Compass**: Provides ethical perspective, questions dubious choices
- **Comic Relief**: Lightens tension, makes endearing mistakes
- **Mysterious Guide**: Provides cryptic hints, has hidden knowledge
- **Traumatized Companion**: Triggered by trauma, grows through arc
- **Practical Skeptic**: Questions plans, points out risks, ultimately supports

**Step 3: Select Genre Template** (Chapter 40)
- **Cozy**: Helpful neighbor's child, loyal pet, wise elder
- **Fantasy**: Oath-bound guardian, spirit familiar, apprentice
- **Horror**: Haunted survivor, protective ghost, AI monitor
- **Modern**: Partner/driver, informant, bodyguard
- **Sci-Fi**: Starship AI, alien symbiote, android crew member

**Step 4: Define Limitations**
- What can't or won't the VPC do?
- How do they stay complementary, not competitive?
- When should they fail or be absent?

**Step 5: Set Exit Strategy**
- How does VPC gracefully exit when no longer needed?
- What completes their character arc?

**Example**: Kess the field medic (Loyal Protector script). Skills: Medicine, First Aid, Perception. Gear: 3 medical kits per session. Limitations: won't abandon wounded PCs, limited combat ability. Exit: stays in safe town as medical contact once PCs learn medicine.

## Enemy Quick Creation {#enemy-quick-creation}

Create memorable enemies with motivations, weaknesses, and tactics.

### Enemy Creation Steps {#enemy-creation-steps}

**Step 1: Define Motivation** (Chapter 41)
- Why do they oppose PCs? (resources, ideology, fear, orders, nature)
- What do they want to achieve?

**Step 2: Choose Enemy Type** (Chapter 41)
- **Minion**: Numerous, low threat individually, weak morale, simple tactics (1-2 skills)
- **Elite**: Skilled, moderate threat, adaptable tactics, distinct personality (3-4 skills)
- **Boss**: Major threat, complex motivations, multiple approaches (4-6 skills)
- **Environmental Threat**: Non-sapient danger using tags and clocks

**Step 3: Define Weakness** (Chapter 41)
- **Tactical**: Predictable patterns, exploitable fighting style
- **Narrative**: Family, honor code, limited resources
- **Psychological**: Pride, fear, obsession

**Step 4: Select Tactics** (Chapter 41)
- **Aggressive**: Direct assault, overwhelming force
- **Defensive**: Hold position, protect objective
- **Cunning**: Ambush, misdirection, exploitation
- **Desperate**: Reckless attacks, sacrifice allies

**Step 5: Add Skills and Environmental Synergy** (Chapter 41)
- Choose skills appropriate to enemy type and tier
- Consider how enemy uses terrain, tags, and conditions
- Add genre-appropriate tags

**Example**: Bandit Leader (Elite, Tier 2). Motivation: profit through theft. Weakness: negotiable, flees when losses mount. Tactics: ambush from prepared positions, demands surrender. Skills: Archery, Intimidation, Tactics, Survival. Tags: Opportunistic, Armed.

## Quick Tips for GMs {#quick-tips}

**NPC Creation**:
- Personality over stats. A memorable mannerism beats detailed mechanics.
- Every NPC wants something and hides something.
- Create detail proportional to importance. Don't over-invest in one-scene characters.

**VPC Management**:
- VPCs react, they don't lead.
- They complement, they don't compete.
- Plan their exit from the start.

**Enemy Design**:
- Even minions have motivations.
- Weaknesses create opportunities for clever play.
- Vary tactics to reward player learning.
