# Core Rulebook Blueprint (Updated)

This blueprint reflects the current consolidated structure and numbering, including the dedicated Character Creation chapter as Chapter 6 and the Skills / Proficiencies model we established.

## Part I: Foundations

1. **Welcome to the Game**
   - Purpose of the book
   - What kind of stories the system supports
   - Fiction first philosophy
   - How rules support, not replace, the story

2. **Core Concepts at a Glance**
   - Fiction first
   - Characters as points of view
   - Attributes (MIG, AGI, PRE, RSN) overview
   - Skills and Proficiencies high level
   - Checks and outcome tiers
   - Tags, Conditions, and Clocks as core world and state tools

3. **How to Use This Rulebook**
   - Navigating chapters and sections
   - Rules as tools
   - Fiction first structure
   - Checks in context
   - Learning the flow of the system
   - Using examples and glossary
   - Reading as a group

4. **Core Principles of Play**
   - Table as a creative team
   - Player intent and approach
   - GM honesty and world presentation
   - Failure creates momentum
   - Partial success as a story beat
   - Player agency and clear choices
   - World response to character action
   - Respect for player ownership
   - Scenes with direction
   - Trust and safety at the table

5. **Ways to Play the Game**
   - Group play with a GM
   - Duet play
   - GMless cooperative play
   - Solo play
   - Asynchronous and play by post
   - One shots
   - Campaigns
   - Hybrid styles and transitions

6. **Character Creation**
   - Learning by example note
   - Creation flow overview
   - Step 1: Choose a concept
   - Step 2: Define core identity elements
   - Step 3: Assign Attributes (MIG, AGI, PRE, RSN) with 2 / 1 / 1 / 0 array
   - Step 4: Open lists and collaborative design for Skills / Proficiencies (philosophy section)
   - Step 4: Choose Skills (action based competencies)
   - Step 5: Choose Proficiencies (narrative domain expertise)
   - Step 6: Determine starting gear
   - Step 7: Establish background and relationships
   - Step 8: Define goals, drives, and personal threads
   - Step 9: Final review and campaign fit
   - Full example build: Rella

7. **Characters and Attributes**
   - Deep dive into MIG, AGI, PRE, RSN
   - How Attributes are used with approach and intent
   - Narrative meaning of Attribute ratings
   - Examples by Attribute
   - Attribute growth preview (ties into Advancement chapter)

8. **Actions, Checks, and Outcomes**
   - Core resolution procedure
   - Declaring intent and approach
   - Choosing Attribute and Skill
   - Difficulty ladder
   - Outcome tiers
   - Advantage and disadvantage
   - How Tags, Conditions, and Clocks interact with Checks (forward reference to later chapter)

9. **Tags, Conditions, and Clocks**
   - Definitions and purposes
   - Environmental and situational Tags
   - Character facing Conditions
   - Clock structure and use
   - Examples and guidance for GMs

10. **Combat Basics**
    - Turn structure
    - Positioning and environment
    - Using Attributes and Skills in combat
    - How Tags and Conditions matter
    - Clocks in combat scenes

11. **Exploration and Social Play**
    - Travel, navigation, and discovery
    - Investigation and research
    - Social encounters and negotiations
    - Long term consequences in non combat scenes

12. **Downtime, Recovery, and Advancement Overview**
    - Rest and recovery
    - Downtime activities
    - High level advancement concepts
    - How characters change over time

13. **Roleplaying Guidance and Working with the GM**
    - Playing to find out what happens
