# TTRPG System Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used across all corebooks.

## Core Mechanics

**4d6** {#4d6}
: Core dice mechanic. Roll four six-sided dice, sum all dice, add bonus, compare
to DC. Average roll: 14. Range: 4-24.

**Advantage** {#advantage}
: Roll extra dice and keep best 4. Comes in two levels: +1 Advantage (roll 5d6,
keep best 4) or +2 Advantage (roll 6d6, keep best 4). Granted by favorable
positioning, gear, assistance, or special abilities. Cannot stack beyond ±2
levels.

**Clock** {#clock}
: Progress tracker with segments. Tick forward on successes (Progress Clock) or
escalate pressure (Pressure Clock).

**Condition** {#condition}
: Character status effect (Exhausted, Bleeding, Restrained). Impose mechanical
penalties or narrative limits.

**Critical Failure** {#critical-failure}
: Margin ≤-7 or all 1s rolled. Disaster occurs; worst possible result with major
complication.

**Critical Success** {#critical-success}
: Margin ≥+5. Succeed spectacularly with extra benefit or bonus effect.

**DC (Difficulty Class)** {#dc}
: Target number for checks. Ranges from DC 12 (Easy) to DC 22 (Legendary).
Standard ladder: 12/14/16/18/20/22.

**Disadvantage** {#disadvantage}
: Roll extra dice and keep worst 4. Comes in two levels: -1 Disadvantage (roll
5d6, keep worst 4) or -2 Disadvantage (roll 6d6, keep worst 4). Imposed by poor
positioning, conditions, obstacles, or penalties. Cannot stack beyond ±2 levels.

**Failure** {#failure}
: Margin ≤-3. Goal not achieved; situation escalates or new complication emerges.

**Full Success** {#full-success}
: Margin ≥0. Achieve goal cleanly; tick progress, gain position.

**Margin** {#margin}
: Difference between roll total and DC (Roll - DC). Determines outcome tier.

**Partial Success** {#partial-success}
: Margin -1 to -2. Achieve goal with cost, complication, or reduced effect.

**Proficiency** {#proficiency}
: Narrative permission and mechanical edge in specific area.

**Skill** {#skill}
: Trained competency; grants Advantage or bonuses to relevant checks.

**Tag** {#tag}
: Environmental or situational descriptor (Dim Light, Slick, Cover). Affects
what you can do in a space.

## Attributes

**AGI (Agility)** {#agi}
: Speed, precision, reflexes. Used for dodging, climbing, shooting.

**MIG (Might)** {#mig}
: Physical power, endurance, resilience. Used for lifting, smashing, enduring.

**PRE (Presence)** {#pre}
: Charisma, willpower, influence. Used for persuading, commanding, intimidating.

**RSN (Reason)** {#rsn}
: Logic, knowledge, perception. Used for analyzing, recalling, noticing.

## Character Types

**GM (Game Master)** {#gm}
: Facilitates play, frames situations, adjudicates outcomes.

**NPC (Non-Player Character)** {#npc}
: Character controlled by the GM.

**PC (Player Character)** {#pc}
: Character controlled by a player.

**VPC (Villain/Powerful Character)** {#vpc}
: Major antagonist with advanced mechanics.

## Common Conditions

**Bleeding** {#bleeding}
: Time pressure; condition worsens if not treated. Clear via first aid or rest.

**Dazed** {#dazed}
: Disoriented; Disadvantage on perception and reaction. Cleared after one turn
or aid.

**Exhausted** {#exhausted}
: Reduce effectiveness until rest or aid. Disadvantage on physical actions.

**Exposed** {#exposed}
: Vulnerable; Disadvantage to defense. Clear by moving to cover or breaking line
of sight.

**Frightened** {#frightened}
: Disadvantage on actions while threat is visible. Clear by removing threat or
rallying.

**Marked** {#marked}
: Tracked or targeted; opponent gains Advantage against you. Clear by breaking
contact.

**Poisoned** {#poisoned}
: Ongoing damage or Disadvantage; duration varies. Clear via antidote or medical
aid.

**Restrained** {#restrained}
: Movement limited by bonds, grapple, or obstacle. Clear via help, time, or
leverage.

**Stunned** {#stunned}
: Can't act next turn. Cleared automatically after one turn.

**Suppressed** {#suppressed}
: Disadvantage on next action due to pressure or intimidation. Cleared after
acting.

## Common Tags

**Cramped** {#cramped}
: Hinders movement; increases fall risk; limits large weapons or sweeping
actions.

**Crowded** {#crowded}
: Advantage to blend in or lose pursuit; Disadvantage to chase or spot targets.

**Dim Light** {#dim-light}
: Advantage to stealth; Disadvantage to ranged attacks and detailed observation.

**Elevated** {#elevated}
: Advantage on ranged attacks; forces opponents to climb or find alternate
routes.

**Fragile Cover** {#fragile-cover}
: Grants Advantage to defense but degrades on Partial Success or repeated use.

**Hazardous** {#hazardous}
: Environmental danger (fire, acid, collapsing floor); risk of damage or
conditions.

**Obscured** {#obscured}
: Vision blocked (fog, smoke, darkness); Disadvantage to ranged attacks and
navigation.

**Slick** {#slick}
: Disadvantage on movement and maneuvers requiring balance (ice, oil, wet
stone).

**Solid Cover** {#solid-cover}
: Reliable protection; grants Advantage to defense without degrading.

**Unstable** {#unstable}
: Risk of collapse, shifting terrain, or loss of footing; movement requires
checks.
